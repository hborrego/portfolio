229: Historic Arts and Craft Home Restoration

Made At:

Skacklab Inc.

Role:

Designer and Project Coordinator

When:

2014-2015

Project Details:

229 is a historic Arts and Craft style home built in 1882. It originally functioned as a family and Quaker meeting house before, in more recent years, becoming a bed & breakfast. To modernize the home for a private client, Stacklab assumed responsibility for all levels of design including architecture, interior finishes, custom furnishings and hardware. I was responsible of not only design decisions, but also project coordination with suppliers and contractors during the construction process.

Finding Inspiration in the Existing

During the design process, we found unique design details that were original to the home, which inspired some of the pieces that we created. 3D modeling was an essential tool, for us and the client, that allowed us to visualize and communicate the details faster.

From a Drawer Pulls to Stationary Windows

All aspects where considered and customized based on various meeting with the users. Based on their feedback, we decided to open up the main living/cooking space, since the view towards the lake was a very important feature to highlight, but also was to make the tactile elements, such as a drawer pull, to have a proper feel and look that blend with the existing elements.

These Concepts Were Brought Through the Whole Home
