Workfrom Redesign

Made for:

Workfrom

Role:

Product Designer

When:

2019

Project Details:

Workfrom is a product that allows users to find cafes, coffee shops or co-working spaces where they can work from. The collection of available places focuses on the immediate needs to the user, such as internet connection or noise preference. Workfrom removes the extra steps and uncertainty by simplifying the process to finding the perfect place to work from in an unfamiliar place. The product is perfect for people who want to explore a new neighborhood in their city or travelers trying to get work done in a new place. Workfrom gives solutions so they can focus on work rather than where to work from.

Initial Research

In our initial user interviews, we observed that people were attracted by the idea to find a place, but were disappointed with the experience of the product. In particular, users manifested discontent by the lack of intuitiveness that the product has. Common insights, such as saturated results, lack of refined suggestions, absence of personal preferences, and inconsistent UI language. Additionally, users expressed an special interest on how the product could be useful in unknown scenarios, where they don't know where is north or south, for instance, which is a feature that the product lacks.

We decided to concentrate in the three the most notable issues.

Issue 01: Difficulty Deciding Which Cafe is Optimal

Intention

Use the map to locate the most suitable working space based on current location.

Current Difficulty

Lack of hierarchy and saturated results make it hard to choose an optimal place for the user.

Strategy

Reduce the amount of options by filtering results that do not fulfill users expectations.

Issue 02: Internet Connection Should be Part of the Decision Making Process

Intention

Display if working spaces have good internet connection or not.

Current Difficulty

The internet speed is hard to find and does not make part of the discovery experience.

Strategy

Bring internet speed as a critical aspect early in the discovery experience.

Issue 03: Orientation in an unknown place

Intention

Give clear directions to the user in a unknown area.

Current Difficulty

The product does not offer any alternative other than sending the user to an external map app.

How to improve it?

Integrate directions and orientation strategies within the product.

Getting to know the user from the beggining

Location and general preferences give control to the user to define the kind of place that they are looking to work from.

Filtered Results and Valuable Information as Part of the Discovery Process

Valuable information are displayed during the discovery process. Rather than using technical terminology, such as "30mbps" to define the internet speed, a more relatable language was chosen.
