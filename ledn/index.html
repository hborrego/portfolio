Ledn: Loan Repayments

Made for:

Ledn Inc, a Canadian company that offers world-class financial services, such as savings or loans accounts, catered for digital assets — aka. cryptocurrencies.

Role:

Product Designer

Techniques:

Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Handoff

When:

2019-2020

Project Overview:

Ledn offers loans backed by crypto holdings. The loan product is popular among users who want access to their holdings without the need to sell their digital assets.Although the company was in the mission to make crypto more accesible for new adopters and existing users, the experience was confusing. I worked on the improvement to make baking with crypto easier and more familiar within the Leid Account Platform.

Problem Definition

Example of the existing loan repayment experience by liquidating Bitcoin collateral.

The main issue with the product was the repayment process, which was handled manually by the reps at Ledn. through Email, checking bank transactions, selling assets, and cross checking information. The lack of automation was inefficient and prompt to human error, plus it created major overhead for the company. For users, the repayment process was a challenging experience as some of the quotes needed to be fulfilled within a certain timeframes using irritating ways of communication.

How might we streamline the repayment process in a way that allows users to make loan payments in a more approachable environment?

Discovery

I worked among with a UX Researcher and the Product Owner during the initial discovery state. we reseached the problem space, framed the challanges to be solved and gathered the evidence, which guided our initial approach on the challanges with the loan repayment system.

Our methodology included:

  • User Interviews (8 Users)
  • Staff Interviews (4 Members)
  • Surveys (Email)
  • Competitive Analysis (4 Competitors)

Findings and Analysis

One

Problem: Lack of Visibility of Loan Status

Users struggle to understand the LTV (Loan-to-Value) ratio, which is the indicator if a loan is healthy or not, based on bitcoin price and amount owed.

Strategy: Highlight a Dynamic LTVs Status

Users wanted more automated and flexible forms of repayment, such as using direct deposits from their bank account or even liquidate their bitcoin as form of payment.

Two

Problem: Deficiency of Payment Options

Users wanted more automated and flexible forms of repayment, such as using direct deposits from their bank account or even liquidate their bitcoin as form of payment.

Strategy: Automate and Create More Repayment Options

This not only would help aliviate the frustrations with the current repayment system based on messaging back and forward but would also help the business overhead.

Three

Problem: Current Interface is Unfriendly

Users wanted a more inviting and friendlier dashboard experience — the existing interface intimidated users as there was little explanation how to use all the available tools while repaying.

Strategy: Create an Easy to Understand Journey

Some of the financial jargon showed to be very challenging for the persona that we were targeting. Using natural language and graphics that were easier to understand became a key priority for us.

Ideation

1: Highlight a Dynamic LTVs Status

We added more visible LTV statuses at strategic points when the user access their account. These inclided:

  • Layout Hirearchy
  • In-App Notifications
  • Push Notifications

2: Automate and Create More Repayment Options

Based on the data gathered we automated the liquidation of bitcoin collateral as a way of payment for loans. This gave flexibility for users who have most of their assets in bitcoin and want to take advantage of it. We also improved the Interact E-Transfer (Canada only) and bank wire experience.

3: Create an Easy to Understand Journey

Crypto is hard enough to understand it. The overall tone of voice was revisited to remove any complicated jargon.
