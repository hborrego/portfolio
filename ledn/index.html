Ledn Platform: Loan Repayments

Made at

Ledn Inc

Role

Product Designer

Techniques

Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Handoff

When

2019-2020

Project Overview

Ledn is a Canadian company that offers world-class financial services, such as savings and loans accounts, catered for users who are interested in cryptocurrencies. The goal of this project was to research and resolve the existing repayment system for loan products, which are granted to users who want access to their holdings without the need to sell their digital assets. The existing experience was confusing for users and very inneficient for the company to manage. I worked on the improvement to make paying a loan easier to understand for users and more adequate to mantain for employees in the Leid Account Platform.

Problem Definition

Example of the existing loan repayment experience by liquidating Bitcoin collateral.

One of the main issues with the existing loan repayment process was that it was handled mostly manually by staff members at Ledn. For instance, if a user wanted to liquidate the Bitcoin that they used as collateral to get a loan in dollars, they would have to get a quote through Email, which they would have limited time to accept due to bitcoin volatility exchange rates. For users, the repayment process was a challenging experience as some of the quotes needed to be fulfilled within a certain timeframes using irritating ways of communication.

Staff were also responsible of checking bank transactions, selling assets, and cross checking information manually. The lack of automation was inefficient and prompt to human error, plus it created major overhead for the company.

How might we streamline the repayment process in a way that allows users to make loan payments in a more approachable environment?

Discovery

I worked among a UX Researcher and a Product Owner during the initial discovery state. We framed the challanges, which made the current repayment structure suffer the most, conducted research, which refined our focus on what needed our attention the most, and gathered the evidence, which help us gain the confidence that this was a major pain point that needed to be resolved.

Our methodology included:

  • User Interviews
  • Staff Interviews
  • Surveys
  • Competitive Analysis

Findings and Analysis

One

Problem: Lack of Visibility of Loan Status

Users struggle to understand the LTV (Loan-to-Value) ratio, which is the indicator if a loan is healthy or not, based on bitcoin price and amount owed.

Strategy: Highlight a Dynamic LTVs Status

By making the LTV status a priority for users to find, understand and act upon, we could lower user changes of collateral liquidation.

Two

Problem: Deficiency of Payment Options

Users wanted more automated and flexible forms of repayment, such as using direct deposits from their bank account or even liquidate their bitcoin as form of payment.

Strategy: Automate and Create More Repayment Options

This not only would help aliviate the frustrations with the current repayment system based on messaging back and forward but would also help the business overhead.

Three

Problem: Current Interface is Unfriendly

Strategy: Create an Easy to Understand Journey

Some of the financial jargon showed to be very challenging for the persona that we were targeting. Using natural language and graphics that were easier to understand became a key priority for us.

User Flow

Solution

One

Highlight a Dynamic LTVs Status

We added more visible LTV statuses at strategic points when the user access their account. These inclided:

  • Layout Hirearchy
  • In-App Notifications
  • Push Notifications

Two

Automate and Create More Repayment Options

Based on the data gathered we automated the liquidation of bitcoin collateral as a way of payment for loans. This gave flexibility for users who have most of their assets in bitcoin and want to take advantage of it. We also improved the Interact E-Transfer (Canada only) and bank wire experience.

Three

Create an Easy to Understand Journey

Crypto is hard enough to understand it. The overall tone of voice was revisited to remove any complicated jargon.
