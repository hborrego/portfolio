Automating the Loan Repayment Process

Made at

Ledn

Role

Product Designer

Techniques

Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design Handoff

When

2019-2020

Company Overview

Ledn is a Canadian company that offers world-class financial services, such as savings and loans accounts, catered for users who hold cryptocurrencies and want to make the most out of their holdings.

Project Summary

The goal of the project was to research and resolve the existing repayment system for loan products, which are granted to users who want dollars by using their Bitcoin as collateral. The existing experience was confusing for users and very inefficient for the company to manage. I worked on the improvement to make paying a loan easier to understand for users and more adequate to maintain for employees in the Ledn Account Platform.

Problem Definition

Example of the existing loan repayment experience by liquidating Bitcoin collateral.

One of the main issues with the existing loan repayment process was that it was handled mostly manually by staff members at Ledn. For instance, if a user wanted to liquidate the Bitcoin that they used as collateral to get a loan in dollars, they would have to get a quote through Email, which they would have limited time to accept due to bitcoin volatility exchange rates. For users, the repayment process was a challenging experience as some of the quotes needed to be fulfilled within a certain timeframes using irritating ways of communication.

Staff were also responsible of checking bank transactions, selling assets, and cross checking information manually. The lack of automation was inefficient and prompt to human error, plus it created major overhead for the company.

How might we streamline the repayment process in a way that allows users to make loan payments in a more approachable environment?

Discovery

I worked among a UX Researcher and a Product Owner during the initial discovery state. We framed the challenges, which made the current repayment structure suffer the most, conducted research, which refined our focus on what needed our attention the most, and gathered the evidence, which help us gain the confidence that this was a major pain point that needed to be resolved.

Our methodology included:

  • User Interviews
  • Staff Interviews
  • Surveys
  • Competitive Analysis

Findings

Here are the three key issues we identified

1. Fragmented Journey

The existing repayment solution relied on users to access the platform to submit a repayment request and then confirming to a quote via email. There was little explanation how to use all the available payment options, too, which created a frustrating experience when a user was looking for a particular form of payment.

2. Visibility of Loan Status

Users struggled to understand the status of their loan, which was given to them through the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio. For instance, when the LTV is high, it means that the loan could get liquidated unless the user deposits more collateral. The existing LTV didn't give any relevant information, which made it hard for users to make informed decisions based on the number they were looking at.

3. Understanding Quote Confirmation

After users received a loan repayment quote through email, they struggled to understand the format of the quote. There was no hierarchy and most users often had to use a calculator to understand which number was doing what. This often made it harder for some users to even respond and confirm repayment.

Solutions

How the three key issues were addressed

1. Fragmented Journey

We remapped the loan journey from scratch. We reduced the amount of unnecessary options and simplified the choices based on the loan status. We also implemented different automated repayment options, which allow the users to get the repayment process completely done without leaving the Ledn Platform.

2. Visibility of Loan Status

We made the Loan-To-Value (LVT) field part of the main loan information hierarchy, which allows users to quickly understand the status of their loan and act upon it if needed. The LVT field also uses color and caption messages to make sure the user lower their risk of getting their collateral liquidated.

3. Understanding Quote Confirmation

We reduced the amount of information and organized it in a layout, which helps to understand in simple math how the repayment process is getting done. This demystified the complex repayment options, such as when the user decided to sell part the collateral to pay for a loan.
