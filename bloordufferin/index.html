Bloor & Dufferin

Made At:

Giannone Petricone Associates

Role:

Designer: 3D Modeler and Visualization Strategist

When:

2018-2019

Project Details:

I made part of the team that conceptualized and designed the user experience and visual strategy at ground level for the Bloor & Dufferin development proposal in Toronto, Canada. I was responsible of creating 3D models and strategize the rendering representations used to communicate the general design intent to future users.

A Space Where People's Experiences Come First

The vision for Bloor & Dufferin parted with the idea of creating a new destination point in the city, for locals and visitors alike. The project approach is based on community-oriented, pedestrian-first design decisions that aim to create new public spaces, boutique shopping experiences, a new community hub, increased accessibility, a neighborhood daycare, reenvisioned heritage spaces and new park areas. The ultimate goal is to bring vibrant new textures to the neighborhood’s fabric.

Designs Decisions by the Community, for the Community

The proposal evolved and the initial approach changed, based on community feedback from the different stakeholders, including neighbors and potential new residents, which ultimately inspired proposals for the benefit of all, such as:

  • A 35,000 sq. ft. Community Hub
  • Best-in-Class Daycare
  • 38,500 sq. ft. of New Park Space
  • 44% New Public Space
  • Pedestrian-Oriented Design
  • Opportunities for Entrepreneurs
  • Reenvisioned Heritage Spaces
  • Affordable Housing
