Bloor & Dufferin Development

Made At:

Giannone Petricone Associates

Role:

Designer: 3D Modeler and Visualization Strategist

When:

2018-2019

Project Details:

I made part of the team that designed the user experience at ground level and visual strategy for the Bloor & Dufferin development proposal in Toronto, Canada. I was responsible of creating 3D models and strategize the visual representations that expressed the design intent to future users.

A Space Where People's Experiences Come First

The vision for Bloor & Dufferin parted with the idea of creating a new destination point in the city for locals and visitors. The project approach is based on community-oriented, pedestrian-first design decisions that aim to create new public spaces, boutique shopping experiences, a new community hub, increased accessibility, a neighborhood daycare, reenvisioned heritage spaces and new park areas. The goal is to bring vibrant new textures to the neighborhood’s storied fabric.

Designs Decisions by the Community, for the Community

The proposal evolved and continued to changed based on feedback received from the different stakeholders, including community neighbors and potential new residents. Based on this, the development proposes:

  • A 35,000sq.ft. Community Hub
  • Best-in-Class Daycare
  • 38,500sq.ft. of New Park Space
  • 44% New Public Space
  • Pedestrian-Oriented Design
  • Opportunities for Entrepreneurs
  • Reenvisioned Heritage Spaces
  • Affordable Housing
